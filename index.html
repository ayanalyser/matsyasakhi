About MatsyaSakhi

MatsyaSakhi is a revolutionary social enterprise that empowers women in India's fishing communities through comprehensive training, cooperative ownership models, and ethical seafood processing. Our mission is to uplift 10,000 women fishers by 2030, driving inclusive growth in the blue economy.

We produce hygienic dried fish, pickles, and snacks while supporting women-led fisheries through a dual model of business for revenue and an NGO for training and grants.

Our initiative blends the vision of MatsyaSakhi with the cultural and economic realities of coastal communities, creating a women-led fisheries model that serves both B2B and D2C markets.

Our Core Innovations

Off-grid storage with real-time monitoring, reducing spoilage from 30% to less than 10%.

QR Traceability

Scan to see catch location, producer details, and certifications for complete transparency.

Social Impact

Empowering Coastal Women

MatsyaSakhi addresses low wages in post-harvest fisheries, where women often earn only ₹100–200 per day without job security.

Through our initiative, women move from low-paid, unstable work to steady, better-paying roles in value-added fish processing with:

  • More control over money through SHG-based cooperative ownership
  • Safer, cleaner workspaces with proper hygiene standards
  • More women taking leadership roles in the fisheries sector
  • Access to bigger markets - from local shops to exports and online sales

We partner with NABARD and SIDBI to facilitate micro-loans and improve access to credit.

Beneficiary Distribution

Primary Beneficiaries (45-50%)
Secondary Beneficiaries (30-35%)

Primary Beneficiaries: Women from the Mogaveera community involved in fishing, processing, and selling activities.

Secondary Beneficiaries: Male members of fishing households gaining from increased family income and reduced post-harvest losses.
